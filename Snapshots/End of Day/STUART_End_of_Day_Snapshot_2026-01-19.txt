ARCHITECTURAL CLARIFICATIONS LOCKED DURING EXECUTION
================================================================================

1) DOMAIN-BASED MODULE NAMING (CRITICAL)

Decision:
- Modules are named after DOMAINS, not AGENTS.

Therefore:
- Stuart (agent) inhabits the 'meetings' domain.
- The module remains ashby/modules/meetings/.
- We do NOT rename the module to 'stuart' in this raid.

Rationale:
- Ashby routes by domain intent (meetings.*, lights.*, etc.).
- Agent identity is layered on top of domain modules.
- This prevents premature abstraction and routing ambiguity.

2) MODULE REMOVABILITY (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Modules are removable organs.

Removing a module means:
- Delete ashby/modules/<domain>/
- Remove the module’s registry entry (once registry wiring exists)
- Ashby core continues operating without that capability

Artifact behavior on removal:
- Artifacts produced by the module (e.g., Stuart files under STUART_ROOT)
  persist on disk by default.
- Interpreting or generating new artifacts requires the module.
- Data purge is an explicit, separate operation.

This enables:
- Shipping Ashby with or without Stuart
- Shipping Ashby with or without future modules (e.g., cameras/AshEyes)
- No platform refactors when modules are added or removed
