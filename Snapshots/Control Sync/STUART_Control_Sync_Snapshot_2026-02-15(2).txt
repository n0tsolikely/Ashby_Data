REV3 UPDATE — FIELD TEST FINDINGS (GOVERNANCE vs NO GOVERNANCE) + INCUBATION HARDENING NOTES
Timestamp (America/Toronto): 2026-02-15 17:20:02 EST
===============================================================================

FINDINGS / OBSERVATIONS (High Signal)
- Controlled comparison (same task: "YouTube → MP4 web app") showed a stark behavior change:
  - WITHOUT governance: model immediately started building, did not ask clarifying questions, hallucinated defaults, and entered repeated error/patch loops.
  - WITH governance: model initiated structured exploration, asked iterative clarifying questions in small batches ("need 3 things", then "need 2 more"), explained unknown terms, and only proceeded once requirements were explicit enough to generate a TOC and codex. Outcome: working app with minimal/no patch churn.
- Governance forced "clarify-first" behavior via incubation → TOC → codex pipeline. This prevented gap-filling hallucinations and reduced error loops.
- Real-user simulation: Hands intentionally avoided "coaching" the AI on governance mechanics (no "only execute one quest at a time", no "remember to snapshot") to test whether Synapse works for a brand-new user.
- In that test, the AI:
  - Built TOC, codex subsections, guild orders, and quests correctly.
  - Executed quests successfully (but batch-executed multiple in one output). For small apps this still worked; for larger apps it becomes a scaling boundary requiring explicit chunking/throttling rules.

DECISIONS / HARDENING TARGETS (Binding for tomorrow’s work)
1) Incubation phase must preserve Discoveries during long sessions WITHOUT requiring Hands to prompt "save discoveries".
   - The Draftshot mechanism is the selected continuity anchor to solve long-session capture.
2) TOC + Codex self-check gates must be strengthened:
   - After TOC generation: AI must self-assess whether TOC covers "every base" as if the product already exists fully.
   - During codex writing (each subsection): AI must self-check for ambiguity and missing requirements.
   - After full codex: AI must self-check "written as if the idea already exists" (zero unanswered questions; no guessing needed by a fresh AI in a new chat).
   - If gaps are found: AI must add missing sections/clarifications BEFORE freezing/codex-finalization.

FINDINGS / OBSERVATIONS (Meta)
- Hands used minimal interventions (two notable ones):
  - Requested discovery saving after noticing closure didn’t preserve them (builder instinct). This is now to be eliminated via governed protocol.
  - Requested an honest "is this enough / any gaps" check after codex completion; AI reported "close but gaps" and appended missing items. This check should become a standard gate, not an ad-hoc prompt.

  - Draftshot-compatible Discovery preservation during long incubation/control sync sessions.
  - Codex/TOC sufficiency self-check gates (pre-freeze and post-freeze behaviors).
