──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1) WHAT ASHBY IS (LOCKED)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ashby is NOT a chatbot, assistant, or single-purpose product.

Ashby is an AGENTIC PLATFORM — an operating layer whose job is to:
- turn intelligence (NLU + reasoning) into reliable, stateful action
- coordinate many systems, devices, services, and agents
- remember context and preferences
- enforce truth (never lie about actions or state)
- scale without collapsing into a god-file

Smart home control is ONE product profile of Ashby, not its definition.
Meetings (Stuart), inventory (Tori), media generation, and future agents are modules
built on the same skeleton.

Ashby is the brain. Plumbing is replaceable.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2) NON‑NEGOTIABLE PRINCIPLES (LOCKED)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
These principles are immutable unless a future Control Sync explicitly changes them:

- Engine is sacred: no vendor logic, no optional features, no products.
- WHAT vs HOW is absolute:
    WHAT → modules
    HOW  → adapters / bridges / native_vendor / connectors
- Modules are fully removable.
- Scopes/zones are universal and Ashby-owned.
- Action‑Gated Truth is mandatory:
    Ashby never claims an action occurred unless execution confirms it.
- HouseState confidence (VERIFIED / LAST_KNOWN / UNKNOWN) is authoritative.
- Control Sync governs structural change.
- Products are profiles + feature flags, not code forks.

4) HOME ASSISTANT, ZIGBEE, MATTER — FINAL DECISIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Home Assistant:
- Is a TEMPORARY INTEGRATION BRIDGE.
- Legal to bundle and ship inside Ashby hardware.
- Invisible to end users.
- Absorbs vendor OAuth, discovery, and protocol pain.
- NOT Ashby’s identity.
- Intended to be gradually replaced over time.

Zigbee and Matter:
- Are PROTOCOLS, not brands.
- Reduce vendor friction.
- Can be supported via Home Assistant first.
- Later supported natively by Ashby.
- Helpful accelerators, not defining features.

Long‑term vision:
Ashby will progressively supersede:
- Home Assistant
- Zigbee bridges
- Matter controllers
by implementing native vendor and protocol integrations over time.

Bridges are strangler layers, not permanent dependencies.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6) MODULES & AGENTS (LOCKED)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Modules are WHAT Ashby does. They are removable.

Key modules:
- Lights
- Comfort (thermostat)
- Cameras
- Image Generation (ComfyUI)
- Stuart (Meetings)
- Tori (Inventory)

Stuart:
- Is a module, not a platform.
- Handles recording, diarization, transcription, summarization, search.
- Inherits Ashby’s truth, state, logging, and security.
- May later include built-in recording (no phone required).
- Never bypasses Ashby core.

Tori:
- Is a module.
- Handles inventory, ordering, tracking, reconciliation.
- Leverages connectors (commerce, docs, etc.).

Multi-agent coexistence is solved:
Ashby is the body. Agents are organs. Truth is the nervous system.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7) ZONES / SCOPES (LOCKED)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Scopes (zones) are canonical and Ashby-owned.

- Devices attach to zones.
- Rooms are human labels mapped to zones.
- Zones stack (bedroom → upstairs → house).
- No adapter or vendor defines zones.

Ashby can say:
“Turn off upstairs lights”
and resolve correctly regardless of backend.
